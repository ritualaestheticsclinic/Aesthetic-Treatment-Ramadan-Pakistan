Aesthetic Treatments Halal Pakistan: The Islamic Perspective

Faith & Aesthetics

A commonly held view among many Islamic scholars is that topical and non-invasive aesthetic treatments do not invalidate the fast. The basis for this is that nothing enters the body through ingestion. Treatments like laser hair removal, facials, RF microneedling, and chemical peels are applied externally, and this is why most scholars in Pakistan consider them permissible during fasting hours.

That said, every patient's personal comfort matters most. If you have any doubt, please do consult your own religious advisor before proceeding. Ramadan Skincare Islamabad: Simple Tips to Keep Your Skin Healthy

Your Ramadan skincare routine does not need to be complicated, but it does need to be intentional. The window between iftar and sehri is your best opportunity to hydrate your skin from the inside out. Drink plenty of water during these hours, as this is the most direct way to counteract dehydration effects on skin that build up through the day.

Use a gentle, hydrating cleanser morning and evening. Many patients switch to a richer moisturiser during Ramadan to compensate for the dryness that fasting can bring. A nourishing serum at night, particularly one with hyaluronic acid, works well alongside whatever in-clinic treatment you are continuing.

Ramadan Skincare Tip

Do not skip SPF during Ramadan. Even in the holy month, UV exposure causes pigmentation and ageing. A light, non-comedogenic sunscreen takes thirty seconds to apply and makes a real difference to your skin over the course of the month.

At iftar, reach for antioxidant-rich foods such as tomatoes, berries, nuts, and leafy greens. These support your skin's natural repair processes overnight, which is especially helpful if you are in the middle of a treatment course. A consistent Ramadan skincare routine paired with professional treatment is a powerful combination for Islamabad patients who want real results.
